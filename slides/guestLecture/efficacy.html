What Political Involvements Change

Political involvement:

All types of voluntary public participation in political affairs coming into contact with government institutions and their agents (Verba and Nie 1987).

Known

  • Experiences shape attitudes and behaviors (Bechler, Tormala, and Rucker 2021; Fazio, Zanna, and Cooper 1978)

Don’t know

  • How
    • What aspects are shaped
    • How guiding future decisions

Crucial to understanding the mechanisms of opinion formation and predicting outcomes of political participation

Efficacy: Linking the experience and attitudes

  • Efficacy as the link
    • Efficacy → opinions/attitudes (Velasquez and LaRose 2015; Geurkink et al. 2020; Sulitzeanu-Kenan and Halperin 2013; Anderson 2010)
    • Behavior, interest, identity, SES, network… → efficacy (Levy 2018; Karv, Lindell, and Rapeli 2022; Pei, Pan, and Skitmore 2018; Kudrnáč and Lyons 2018)

Studies have shown that internal and external efficacy have separate but significant effects on citizens’ immediate political reactions, like political emotions (Velasquez and LaRose 2015), and on their long-term views of the government and regime, such as populist attitudes (Geurkink et al. 2020), polarized ideology(Sulitzeanu-Kenan and Halperin 2013), and political trust(Anderson 2010).

Researchers have found that GPE can reflect a series of sociopolitical factors, many of which are closely related to various levels of political experiences. For instance, at the individual level, higher GPE is found to be associated with participation in real political processes, political interest, and political identity recognition(see a summary in Levy 2018). At the social level, higher GPE is connected with social status, civic resources, and political socialization (Karv, Lindell, and Rapeli 2022). Likewise, cross-pressures and social networks shape GPE (Pei, Pan, and Skitmore 2018; Kudrnáč and Lyons 2018). Lastly, at the institutional level, research has found that citizens’ GPE levels reflect characteristics of socioeconomic systems (e.g., the education system) and political institutions (e.g., electoral forms and governmental transparency) (Khoo, Selvanathan, and Lickel 2021; Cicatiello, De Simone, and Gaeta 2018).

Low-politics online involvement

OPE (vs. GPE): Internal/external efficacy empowered by the Internet

Hypotheses

\(H_{1.1}\)(Impression effect): A more intense government response boosts citizens’ external OPE.
\(H_{1.2}\)(Confidence effect): A smaller gap between the government’s actual response and citizens’ prior expectations increases citizens’ internal OPE.

\(H_{2.1}\): Impression effect → expectations ↑.
\(H_{2.2.0}\): Confidence effect ⥇ expectations, but preferences
\(H_{2.2.1}\): Confidence effect → expectations ↑ < impression effect.

\(H_{3.0}\): Impression/confidence effects → OPE; ⥇ GPE.
\(H_{3.1}\): Impression → external GPE ↑, and a higher confidence effect → internal GPE ↑.

\(H_{1.1}\)(Impression effect): A more intense government response boosts citizens’ external OPE.
\(H_{1.2}\)(Confidence effect): A smaller gap between the government’s actual response and citizens’ prior expectations increases citizens’ internal OPE.

\(H_{2.1}\): The impression effect leads to higher expectations of the government in future demand-response interactions.
\(H_{2.2.0}\): The confidence effect does not modify expectations, but reinforces preferences for institutional paths.
\(H_{2.2.1}\): The confidence effect increases expectations of the government, but to a smaller degree than the impression effect increases expectations.

\(H_{3.0}\): The impression and confidence effects only affect OPE not GPE.
\(H_{3.1}\): A higher impression effect leads to a higher external GPE, and a higher confidence effect leads to a higher internal GPE.

Experimental Design

Scenario

最近，本市市民孙先生遇到了这样的情况： 一日晚间9点左右有男性人员上面查当月水表。该人留有长发，未戴口罩、鞋套，见开门就想进屋。孙先生立即制止，质询其为何没带口罩，进屋也不穿鞋套，请其装备齐后改日再来。该人员称白天已来了多次，都没有人。经交涉，该人员最终同意不进屋亲自查表，由孙先生进屋拍照出示给他进行记录。 事后，孙先生抱怨说，“平时白天大人小孩儿都在上班上学，非那个时候来，当然没有人。下班后来吧，派这么打扮的男的来，还来这么晚，亏得我在家了，要是只有我老婆和孩子在，这有多大安全隐患呀！更何况还不戴口罩、不穿鞋套，这不是给疫情防治添乱么！我觉得就不该招这样的人上门查水表，而且现在微信这么发达了，到时拍张照，按数字交不就完了，根本不用上门查表。” 请您以对现实的了解为基础回答下列问题，而非选出您所期待的情况。

最近，本市市民王女士也遇到了一些情况： 她所住单元楼一层楼下的店面在一楼与二楼之间搭出一个5平米左右的平台用来遮雨，时间已经很久了，钢架材质已经生锈，而且每逢刮风下雨都会出现噪音。 王女士不堪其扰，与楼下店主商议，对方以拆除耽误做生意为由，拒不拆除。王女士认为，这个遮雨台不止扰民而且具有安全隐患，是违法建筑。

Treatment

  • 政务平台受理了孙先生的投诉，报给水务局，但再也没有后续信息。 1
  • 政务平台受理了孙先生的投诉，报给水务局。水务局联系孙先生，责成当地物业对该查表人员进行批评教育。政务平台对孙先生对投诉处理结果满意度进行了回访。 2
  • 政务平台受理了孙先生的投诉，报给水务局。水务局联系孙先生，责成当地物业为孙先生片区更换查表员。政务平台对孙先生对投诉处理结果满意度进行了回访。 3
  • 政务平台受理了孙先生的投诉，报给水务局。水务局联系孙先生，责成当地物业为孙先生片区更换查表员，并针对此案例对全体查表人员进行教育。政务平台对孙先生对投诉处理结果满意度进行了回访。 4
  • 政务平台受理了孙先生的投诉，报给水务局。水务局联系孙先生，责成当地物业为孙先生片区更换查表员，并针对此案例对全体查表人员进行教育，且出台住户预约查表时间和网络提交查表记录制度。政务平台对孙先生对投诉处理结果满意度进行了回访。 5

Measurement

Pre-test

  • Evaluation
    • OPE: Internal & external (1–10)
    • GPE: Internal & external (1–10)
    • Expectation of response (1–5)
  • Suggestion
    • Path to response (Type 1–9)

Post-test

  • Evaluation
    • OPE

Path suggestion

  1. 不上报这件事，避免给自己找麻烦
  2. 电话或微信联系物业或居委会
  3. 使用市政务门户网站互动交流平台
  4. 拨打12345市民服务热线投诉
  5. 查找所属社区网格员，通过网格系统反映情况
  6. 直接到水务局服务大厅反应情况
  7. 和其他家人或朋友进一步讨论一下决定是否投诉
  8. 联系本地媒体，通过媒体手段引起相关部门的重视
  9. 将个人经历发布到网络上，通过网络舆论引起相关部门的重视

Follow-up test

    • OPE: Internal & external (1–10)
    • GPE: Internal & external (1–10)
    • Expectation of response (1–5)
  • Suggestion
    • Path to response (Type 1–9)

Data Collection

  • Pre-registered experiment
  • “Online Behavior Survey of Chinese Youth” (OBSCY) in 2020
    • Youth? (Lee, Chang, and Hur 2021; Martin, Martins, and Naqvi 2018)
    • Quota sampling, adjusted by education and gender from the census
      • 189 cities (excluding Tibet, Hong Kong, Macao, and Taiwan)
  • Attention check: all respondents using fewer than 10 mins were removed
  1. Common pool
  2. Not affected by pre-experiment experiences
  3. Major target of political campaigns, mobilizations, and online activities—already high efficacy.

Conclusion

  • Political experiences influences political cognitions through OPE
    • Both work, while the impression effect is more influential.
  • Implications
    • Direct causal evidence of how involvements shape thoughts
    • OPE Vis-à-vis GPE
