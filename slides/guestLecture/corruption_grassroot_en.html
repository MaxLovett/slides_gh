Methodological Puzzle

Solving the substantive Puzzles

  1. Survey respondents will under-report their bribery behavior due to social desirability.
  2. Those who work and live in places closer to the bureaucratic system are less likely to be bribers due to the ability of the government to crackdown on corruption and their easier access to public services.
  3. Political mobilization reduces grassroots bribery (the very point to report them)
  4. The existence of an interpersonal network encourages bribery
  5. The long-term impact of economic growth reduces grassroots bribery, while short-term effect of economic growth encourages bribery.

Solving the Methodological Puzzle

List experiment, endorsement experiment, randomized response technique (Rosenfeld, Imai, and Shapiro 2016; Blair, Imai, and Lyall 2014)

Limitation and Extension

Pros

  1. True answers
  2. Measuring desirability
    • W. direct questions

Cons

  1. Within experiment
  2. Aggregate measurement

Extension

  • Multiple questions
  • Weighting the respondents \[\begin{align} O^{type} =& \frac{Pr^{type}_{list}}{Pr^{type}_{direct}},\\ W_i =& \sum I_i^{type}\cdot\frac{O^{type}}{t_i}. \end{align}\]

\(I_i^{type}\): 0/1, whether i report;

\(O^{type}\): Experiment/direct;

\(t_i\): Type counts that i reports

Experimental Design

Please think and tell me HOW MANY of these statements you would agree with. Please give me a number from 0 to 4, and DO NOT mention any specific item on the list.

Control:

  1. I know the name of the head of the Central Bank of China
  2. I watch TV every day
  3. I own a cell phone
  4. The pensions in China are high enough

Treatment

  1. Over the last two years, I presented an informal payment/gift/did a favor in addition to a required formal fee in exchange for their services to government employees in
    • Health care (T 1)
    • Education (T 2)
    • Local government offices (T 3)
    • Public security and law (T 4).
  • 1993-1995： 朱镕基
  • 2018：周小川/易纲
  • 2023: 潘功胜

Delicate Design

  • Prevent ceiling and floor Effect
  1. I know the name of the head of the Central Bank of China (-)
  2. I watch TV every day (?)
  3. I own a cell phone (+)
  4. The pensions in China are high enough (?)
  5. I presented an informal payment/gift/did a favor (?)

    • List - Direct = (Average) Social Desirability

Corrected Perception

Seeking the Source

  • Institutional: Urban/rural (-), migrant (+), Party-state job (-), city level of residence (U-shape)
  • Political mobilization: TV exposure (-), social media exposure (+)
  • Culture: Interpersonal trust (-)
  • Economic development:
    • Long term: Education, family income level (-)
    • Short term: Retrospective & prospective family economy (+)
  • Controls: Gender, age, local anti-corruption
  • Institutional: Distance to the bureaucratic system (higher ability to crackdown corruption + being easier to access public sources)
    • 1)tier 1 cities of large cosmopolitan centers such as Beijing, 2)provincial capital cities, 3)prefecture cities, and 4)-6)high, middle and low-income counties and county-level cities.
  • Eco development: offering disposal money
    • Long term reduces
    • Short term increases
