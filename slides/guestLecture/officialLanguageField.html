Ethnolinguistics OLF

Direction Integrated Functionally divergent

In an official language field,

  • Listening proficiency ↑ → political political knowledge + Alginment ↑ → political trust ↑

  • Speaking proficiency ↑ → internal efficacy ↑ + external efficacy ↓ → political trust ↓

  • Relative proficiency ↑ → gratification ↑ + social perception ↑ → political trust ↑

  • \(H_1\): citizens with higher listening proficiency in the official language are likely to possess more accurate political knowledge about the established government and exhibit a greater willingness to maintain alignment with it. Consequently, they tend to hold higher levels of political trust.

  • \(H_2\): In an official language field, citizens with greater speaking proficiency in the official language are more likely to have higher internal political efficacy and lower external efficacy. Both lead to lower political trust.

  • \(H_3\): In an official language field, citizens with higher relative proficiency in the official language experience greater social satisfaction and have a more positive perception of society; their trust in the government is also likely to be higher.
