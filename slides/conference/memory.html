Extensive influence on behaviors and decisions

neuroscience and psychological approaches even further extend the connections between memory and politics, experience and behaviors, and past and present (Fellows 2018; Yamashiro, Van Engen, and Roediger 2022).

Under-researched: A fact + a mechanism

  • The fact: “collective wins”

    • The memory of the society conquered natural or social challenges together.

    • Highlighted by textbooks, entertainment products, memorials, and holidays (Frazer 1999; Ariely 2019)
    • Long term effects: nationalism, exceptionalism, regionlism, etc. (Ariely 2019; Merck, Yamashiro, and Hirst 2020)
  1. Collective wins is very common: it was the U.S., not FDR won the WWII; “We get over it” after disasters
  2. Collective wins were less studied, mostly on value stuff
  • The mechanism:
    • Assuming the past must influence the present (→ Identity & nationalism)
    • Lack of the study of how and an ignorance of the memory holders
