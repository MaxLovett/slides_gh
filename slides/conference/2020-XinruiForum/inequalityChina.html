Two Mechanisms

Some scholars argue that only perceived inequality leads to consequences Gimpelson and Treisman (2018) even stated that most theories about the political effects of inequality need to be reframed as theories about the effects of perceived inequality. Although this literature on perceptions of inequality relaxes one assumption of the OEM that citizens are aware of the objective levels of inequality, it still assumes that citizens consider inequality an important issue when they evaluate their governments.

a less restricted model, i.e., the MEM, that does not rely on people’s perceptions of inequality. political trust decreases in unequal environments because inequalities in economic resources affect how people are treated by governments and how they experience government performance.

Four Paths

Impartial governance

  • The damage of economic disparities on political equality in democracies has been well documented in the literature (Schlozman, Verba, and Brady 2013).
  • Formal and informal institutions exist in non-democracies to convert economic resources into political influence and favorable treatment.
  • Wealthy Chinese private entrepreneurs form extensive connections with local government officials to gain privileges (Chen and Dickson 2010).

Government Non-responsiveness

官职最小的上蔡县令刘题，许是觉得升迁无望，便不问政事，整日饮酒作乐，连一桩命案都懒得过问。结果，这桩命案来头不小，犯案人是田伦的胞姐。因为在上蔡得不到处理，蒙冤的杨素贞到信阳州府衙告状。

  • Substantial evidence indicates that economic inequality reduces social capital and generates distrust and social conflicts.
  • Class conflict theory suggests that in highly unequal societies, people’s perceptions of economic positions on the social ladder are accentuated (Kraus, Tan, and Tannenbaum 2013).
  • Low levels of social capital and accentuated social conflicts make governance difficult as Baldwin and Huber (2010) showed that income differences make an agreement over which public goods to provide more difficult, therefore have a negative relationship with public goods provision (Epp and Jennings 2020).

Judicial Injustice

  1. greater inequality implies that the well-off have more resources to control the legal apparatus while the poor are more disadvantaged.
  2. the advantaged are motivated to attempt such control because of intensified social conflicts.

Gini Coefficient

Path Measurement

  • Impartial governance: To what extent they believed the government treated all citizens equally.
  • Government responsiveness: People’s belief that the government sufficiently responds to the demands of the public.

  • Anti-corruption performance: Satisfaction with their government’s work on anti-corruption.

The responses were gauged on a 1–4 scale with 1 as “totally disagree” and 4 as “definitely agree.”

To examine the risk of the assumption violation, we applied a parametric sensitivity test based on the residual correlation on all the meditative paths (Imai, Keele, and Yamamoto 2010; Imai, Keele, and Tingley 2010; Imai et al. 2011). The test provides a correlation coefficient \(\rho\) with the range of [-1, 1]. A nonzero \(\rho\) indicates the risk of omitted variables that may lead to the assumption violation. The sensitivity test aims to examine how large the \(\rho\) is that may cause an effect no different from zero.

They indicate that the original conclusion about the direction of the effect would maintain unless the \(\rho\)s are above these values. The results of these sensitivity tests imply that the conclusion can be plausible with some departures from the ignorability of the mediator. Note that there is no clear criterion or rule of thumb about how large a \(\rho\) is large enough. Although the value was not as high as in the methodological paper where the sensitivity test was constructed (0.68, Imai, Keele, and Tingley 2010, 64), we see a similarity to some other cases. For instance, in replications published in a top journal paper, the reported \(\rho\)s were 0.2 and 0.39 (Imai et al. 2011). In another top journal publication, the reported \(\rho\) was between 0.09 to 0.22 (Wolak 2020, n. 12).
